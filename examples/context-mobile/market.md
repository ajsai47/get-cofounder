# Market

## Category
Consumer mobile apps — habit tracking and behavior change

## Our Differentiation
Steadily is the only habit tracker built around sustained motivation, not data entry. Streak shields prevent the all-or-nothing psychology that kills most habit attempts. Weekly reflections build self-awareness, not just checkmarks. Mood correlation shows people why habits matter to them personally. We're not the most feature-rich option — we're the one people are still using 30 days later.

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Demographics
- **Role**: Young professional, new parent, career changer — people in life transitions where new routines matter
- **Age**: 24-40, with a sweet spot at 27-34
- **Gender**: 60% female, 40% male (based on beta demographics)
- **Platform**: iPhone primary (70%), Android secondary (30%)
- **Geography**: US, UK, Canada, Australia — English-speaking markets first. Expanding to EU in v2.

### Psychographics
- **Values**: Self-improvement without toxic productivity. Balance. Sustainability over intensity. Privacy.
- **Frustrations**: Downloaded 3 habit apps before, used each for a week, felt guilty about quitting. Overwhelmed by apps with too many features. Annoyed by notifications that feel like nagging.
- **Aspirations**: Build a morning routine that sticks. Drink more water. Read before bed instead of scrolling. Feel like they're making progress, even on imperfect days.

### Behaviors
- **Where they learn**: Instagram (wellness and self-improvement), TikTok (routine/habit content), YouTube (morning routine videos, Huberman Lab clips), podcasts (health and habits genre), Reddit (r/habits, r/getdisciplined)
- **Where they buy**: App Store search ("habit tracker"), recommendations from friends or influencers, TikTok/Instagram "apps I use" posts, listicles ("best habit apps 2026")
- **How they evaluate**: App Store screenshots and reviews, free tier experience (will they hit a paywall in the first session?), first 3 minutes of use (onboarding friction), word of mouth from friends who actually stuck with it

## Distribution Channels
- **App Store Optimization (ASO)**: Primary discovery channel. Title, subtitle, keywords, and screenshots optimized for "habit tracker", "daily habits", "streak tracker", "routine planner". Conversion rate: 34% from listing view to download.
- **Instagram**: Relatable content about building habits. Carousel posts ("5 habits that changed my mornings"), Reels showing the app in use, user-generated content reposts. Growing to 5K followers.
- **TikTok**: Short-form content: "apps that actually changed my life" format, morning routine videos featuring Steadily, habit science explainers. One viral TikTok = 2K downloads in a day.
- **Influencer partnerships**: Micro-influencers (5K-50K followers) in wellness, productivity, and lifestyle niches. Product gifting + affiliate codes. Higher conversion than mega-influencers at 1/100th the cost.
- **Word of mouth**: The accountability partner feature (v1.3) is designed to be a growth loop. Every user who invites a partner doubles the install base organically.
- **PR/press**: Lifestyle publications (Well+Good, Refinery29, Lifehacker) and app review sites. Targeting "best new apps" roundups.

## Market Trends
- **Anti-hustle wellness**: Tailwind — cultural shift away from "rise and grind" toward sustainable self-care. Our gentle approach aligns perfectly.
- **Atomic Habits ecosystem**: Tailwind — James Clear's book sold 15M+ copies. "Habit stacking" and "don't break the chain" are mainstream concepts. People actively searching for tools to implement the book's ideas.
- **App Store subscription fatigue**: Headwind — consumers resist another $5/month subscription. Our freemium model (generous free tier, premium for power features) mitigates this. One-time purchase option under consideration.
- **Screen time awareness**: Headwind — some target users actively reducing app usage. Our Apple Watch + widget strategy (v1.4) addresses this by enabling habit tracking without opening the app.
- **AI-personalized health**: Tailwind — users expect apps to learn their patterns and adapt. Our v2.0 coaching feature positions us here without the "creepy AI" factor.
- **Privacy regulation (GDPR, state laws)**: Tailwind — our privacy-by-default approach is a competitive advantage as data regulations tighten. Competitors with social features and data-heavy models face compliance overhead.
