## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Demographics
- **Role**: Full-stack developer, indie hacker, technical founder
- **Company size**: Solo or 1-3 person team
- **Industry**: Building SaaS products (any vertical)
- **Geography**: Global, English-speaking primary. US, EU, and SEA strongest markets

### Psychographics
- **Values**: Speed, clean code, independence, making money from their skills
- **Frustrations**: Spending weeks on boilerplate, broken tutorials, unmaintained open-source, expensive tools that don't justify the cost
- **Aspirations**: Launch a profitable SaaS, quit their day job eventually, build a portfolio of products

### Behaviors
- **Where they learn**: Twitter, YouTube, Hacker News, dev blogs, Discord servers
- **Where they buy**: Direct from landing page (impulse after seeing a build-in-public update), ProductHunt, GitHub trending
- **How they evaluate**: GitHub stars, recent commits, documentation quality, demo/preview, founder's reputation, Reddit threads comparing options

## Distribution Channels
- **Twitter/X**: Primary channel. Build-in-public content, technical threads, feature announcements. 70% of traffic.
- **Hacker News**: Launch posts and Show HN for major versions. Spiky but high-quality traffic.
- **YouTube**: Tutorial videos and "build a SaaS in X minutes" content. Growing channel.
- **SEO**: Long-tail "nextjs saas boilerplate", "stripe subscription template", "clerk auth starter". Growing.
- **Indie Hackers**: Community posts, milestone sharing. Medium traffic, high conversion.

## Market Trends
- **AI-native development**: Tailwind — developers increasingly use AI to generate code. Tools that work well with AI (typed, well-documented, consistent patterns) win.
- **Solo founder explosion**: Tailwind — more people building products alone. Our entire value prop aligns with this.
- **Framework consolidation around Next.js**: Tailwind — Next.js dominance grows. Being Next.js-first is the right bet.
- **Subscription fatigue**: Headwind — developers increasingly resist subscriptions for developer tools. Our one-time + updates model is a differentiator.
